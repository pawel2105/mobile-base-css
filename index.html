What is this?

This is a boilerplate template forked from Jonathan Verrecchia's responsive template. It takes care of rendering the right layout on various devices based on their viewport sizes, but in addition to that, offers mobile support for devices that have a screen smaller than 480px. The base size starts at 128px and has it's next breakpoints at 240px and 320px.

Work in progress

Please note that this is a work in progress, and a lot of things don't work, or don't work properly just yet. But they will, in time. Be patient.

Only ems

Another thing that differs from the original responsive template is that all sizes are in ems instead of pixels, to ensure fluidity and to use percentages, because that will take care of rendering for edge case screen sizes instead of having to build a zillion different rules.

Multiple stylesheets?

There are mixed opinions about embedded media-queries in a single stylesheet, or having a base stylesheet and linking to others using media-queries if necessary. This template follows the school of the latter. The main reason for this is that the phones this is targeted for have poor connections, and are bandwidth savvy, so splitting requests and only loading the bare necessities takes priority over slightly inconveniencing iPhone and desktop users.

Did you say 128px?!

Why in the world would anyone care about devices that small? Who still has a phone with a 128px wide screen? Actually, quite a lot. The amount of people that have smartphones is tiny compared to the amount of people that have really old, really horrible phones. Those people need a good user experience too.
